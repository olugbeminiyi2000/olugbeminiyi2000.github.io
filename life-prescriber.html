Understanding the project.

About

It's a digital prescription management system at its core. With real-time email reminders, Life Prescriber aims to reduce medication errors and improve patient outcomes.

Problem Statement

Millions of patients worldwide struggle with medication adherence, leading to worsened health outcomes and higher healthcare costs. According to the WHO, medication non-adherence contributes to 125,000 preventable deaths annually in the U.S. alone. Healthcare providers face challenges in effectively monitoring patient adherence and providing timely interventions.

RESEARCH

_______   KEY OBSERVATIONS

Asking the right questions.

Research goals
Our research focused on understanding both the challenges faced by patients with medication adherence and the needs of clinicians in tracking patient compliance. We explored how to build an intuitive system that could provide timely reminders for patients and real-time reports for clinicians.
Patient Experience

Through user research, we discovered that patients struggled with complicated medication management systems that added to their stress, making it harder to stay consistent with their schedules. Many expressed a desire for a simple and intuitive solution that would send reminders without overwhelming them with too much information. Patients wanted a system that would seamlessly integrate into their daily lives, offering gentle nudges to help them stay on track without feeling like an additional burden.

Clinician Experience

The question wasn't just what features we could offer clinicians, but why they would need them in the first place. Clinicians were already overwhelmed with patient data and needed a solution that would streamline medication management without adding to their workload. We spent considerable time exploring how to balance a user-friendly interface with comprehensive features for tracking patient adherence, while ensuring the system remained efficient and effective for busy healthcare professionals.

Software Design

If a software platform isn't intuitive and adaptable, it will struggle to meet the needs of its users. While observing the various tools clinicians use, I realized that software solutions must be designed not only to be functional but also to be seamlessly integrated into their daily workflow. Clinicians should be able to track patient data, adjust medication schedules, and access insights without feeling like they're navigating a complex system. This realization reinforced the importance of a simple yet versatile design that could meet both user needs and technical requirements without overwhelming anyone.

Question areas
I would often engage with clinicians and patients in various healthcare settings to uncover patterns and reveal unknown insights about their medication management needs. This allowed me to better understand the challenges they face and the expectations they have from a system like Life Prescriber. By having answers to questions about user behavior, preferences, and pain points, the team was able to prioritize features that would enhance user experience, ensuring that the product met both practical and emotional needs based on real-world data.

CONCLUSIONS

_______   KEY FINDINGS

Measuring success.

"We wanted to create a solution that empowers individuals to take control of their health and improve their quality of life. Life Prescriber is designed to be that lifeline."

What we did well

I had the privilege of working with an incredibly dedicated and talented team on Life Prescriber. Each engineer brought invaluable expertise, and our frontend developer’s meticulous attention to detail ensured the user interface was both intuitive and responsive. As the lead backend engineer, I focused on optimizing the system’s scalability and functionality, ensuring that the backend supported the user experience. A key strength of our team was our ability to communicate effectively—every meeting was laser-focused on addressing the most pressing challenges. By collaborating closely with our CTO, project manager, and designers, I ensured that every decision was not only innovative but also practical and technically feasible.

Despite the hurdles, the trust and shared commitment within the team kept us focused on delivering a solution that would truly make a difference for individuals managing chronic illnesses.

What I would do differently

When I joined the Life Prescriber team, I quickly worked to understand the project's goals, balancing the backend architecture, integrations with healthcare providers, and scalability. One thing I would focus more on in the early stages is aligning technical constraints with user needs from the start. For example, while working on the backend, I encountered limitations with data synchronization between pharmacies and clinicians that could have been addressed sooner to ensure smoother integrations. In addition to these technical challenges, I also realized the importance of user research.

As a tech-focused team, it’s easy to get caught up in what’s technically feasible rather than deeply understanding the real-world challenges our users face. There were times when I focused more on backend performance rather than building the most user-centric solution. From my experience in engineering-heavy teams, it can sometimes be difficult to find the right balance between technical goals and user experience. I wish I had been more assertive in advocating for a more user-centered approach from the very beginning.
